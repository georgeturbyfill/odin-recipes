Lentil Soup

Description

Lentil soup is a great fantastic choice for a chilly fall day or a cozy winter night. It's warm, filling, and fairly cheap to make. You can even use up soon to expire veggies that you've been ignoring in your fridge. This is easily a one pot meal and can be made fairly quickly. The lentils in this recipe pack a protein punch while the vegetables provide balanced nutrition.

The key to making a delicious lentil soup in the spices. Particularly the cumin, paprika, and coriander. Using expired spices can lead to a disappointment when you sit down to enjoy that first bite.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 celery ribs, chopped
  • 14- ounce can crushed or diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups dry green or brown lentils
  • 7 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 3 cups baby spinach, sliced into ribbons or kale
  • 1 lemon, juiced (about 2 tablespoons)

Instructions

  • Preheat a large stew pot over medium heat and add the olive oil. Begin by adding the garlic, onions, carrots, and celery to the pot and cook for 4-5 minutes.
  • Next, combine all contents of the tomato cans, lentils, vegetable broth, and spices. Allow the aromatics to waft into your nose.
  • Turn the stove to high heat and bring the soup to a boil. Once its reached a boil, simmer with the lid cracked for 30 minutes or until the lentils are soft/tender.
  • Finally, add the spinach and lemon juice and cook until the spinach wilts.

Things to note:

  • Use more water or broth for a thinner soup
  • Shelf life of this soup is 5 days refrigerated.
  • Freeze the soup for longer shelf life.
  • If you like a hardier vegetable than spinach, add kale. Adjust for longer cooking times to soften the kale.
